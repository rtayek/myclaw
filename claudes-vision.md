# Manifold Switchboard Vision

## One sentence

Manifold Switchboard is an AI cockpit you can use without seeing
well or typing well, that remembers everything you have ever asked.

## The problem

AI interfaces assume sharp eyes and fast hands: small text,
mouse-driven layouts, silent responses, and histories scattered
across vendors. Users with low vision or limited typing are
served last.

## The product

One accessible desktop app that talks to many AIs:

- Claude, local Ollama models, and other backends in one
  interface, switchable by keystroke.
- Large, scalable text everywhere.
- Full keyboard navigation; the mouse is optional.
- Speech in: prompt by dictation.
- Speech out: responses read aloud.
- Every conversation saved on your own machine and searchable.
  Nothing leaves your computer except the prompts you send.

## Who it is for

Anyone who needs their ears and voice to do what eyes and hands
usually do. The developer is the first user: this is built from
lived experience, not a compliance checklist.

## First milestone

The developer runs Manifold Switchboard daily and a response can
be read aloud with one keystroke.

## What success looks like

Daily use by the developer and ten real users. Then hundreds to a
few thousand devoted users. Depth over breadth.

## Deferred

Web demo, TV client, teaching features. The core keeps those
doors open; the accessible desktop comes first.

## Guiding principle

If it cannot be done by reading large text, pressing keys, and
speaking, it is not done.
